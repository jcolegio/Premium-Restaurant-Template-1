The Chef

Rosalind Voss

Rosalind trained in wood-fired kitchens across three countries before deciding a menu could be built entirely without gas. Ash & Amber opened in 2019 with a single custom hearth designed around her — everything the kitchen sends out has passed through it.

She still tends the coals herself most nights, and the tasting menu changes whenever a delivery or a forage changes her mind.

A Short History

How the room came to be

2017

The idea

Rosalind begins testing a gas-free menu in a borrowed commissary kitchen after a decade cooking abroad.

2019

Doors open

Ash & Amber opens on Forge Lane with nine tables and a single custom-built hearth.

2022

First accolade

Named "Room of the Year" by the city's dining critics for the tableside coal service.

2026

Today

Still nine tables, still one hearth — booked weeks out, and still changing weekly.

What We Hold To

Four things that don't change

No gas, ever

Every dish passes through the hearth. No exceptions on the line.

One morning's drive

Every ingredient is sourced within a single supply run of the door.

Nine tables only

We've never expanded seating. The room stays the size it was designed for.

The menu moves

Nothing is printed until the week it's served — the fire dictates the plan.
