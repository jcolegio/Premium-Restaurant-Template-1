The Philosophy

Everything touches the fire before it touches the plate.

Our kitchen has no gas line. Every dish begins on a bed of oak and hickory embers, tended by hand through the night — a discipline that trades convenience for depth of flavor you can't fake with a burner.

Chef Rosalind Voss sources within a single morning's drive: one farm for greens, one boat for the day's catch, one forager for what the week allows. The menu is rewritten as often as the season demands it.
